About Me

After completing a Bachelor's degree in Health Information Management at Queensland University of Technology in Australia and a Certificate in Statistical Analysis with R for Public Health, I moved to Canada to pursue a Master of Public Health (MPH) at the Schulich School of Medicine & Dentistry at Western University. While completing my MPH, I also obtained a Data Science Certificate from the University of Waterloo.

Combining my academic achievements and professional background, I have developed a unique and multifaceted skill set that spans the domains of health informatics, clinical coding, epidemiology and public health research. My computer science and statistics proficiency has provided me with a solid foundation for data analysis and machine learning, which I have applied to numerous public health projects.

Driven by a passion for health analytics, epidemiology and quality health improvement, I look to advance my career in this space. My goal is to make meaningful contributions to the field that lead to improved public health outcomes.

Thank you for visiting my website. Please feel free to reach out if you have any questions or if you would like to discuss a potential collaboration.
