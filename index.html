Stroke Prediction

The report describes a project aimed at developing a logistic regression model to predict the likelihood of someone having a stroke. The project involved creating and comparing three models, with the best-performing model using the SMOTETomek technique and including only important features such as age, average glucose level, and BMI.

From Data to Diagnosis: Predicting Heart Health with ML

Cardiovascular diseases claim 17.9 million lives annually, underscoring the need for early detection. Utilizing a Kaggle dataset from a 2020 CDC survey, this project employed machine learning models, with the Support Vector Machine emerging as a top performer, to predict heart disease risks based on lifestyle and risk factors. The findings not only validated the significance of certain risk factors but also highlighted the potential of simpler models in predictive healthcare.
